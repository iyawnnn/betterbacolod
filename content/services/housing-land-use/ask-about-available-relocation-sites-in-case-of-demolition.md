# Relocation Sites and Assistance — Bacolod City

Information about available relocation sites and assistance for families affected by demolition or displacement.

---

## 1. When Relocation is Needed

| Situation             | Reason                                                       |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Government projects   | Road widening, infrastructure development, public facilities |
| Danger zones          | Flood-prone areas, landslide areas, waterways, fault lines   |
| Illegal settlements   | Informal settlers in government or private property          |
| Disaster displacement | Families displaced by fire, typhoon, earthquake              |

---

## 2. Relocation Assistance Available

**From Bacolod Housing Authority (BHA):**

- Relocation to government resettlement sites
- Temporary shelter assistance
- Housing units (for qualified beneficiaries)
- Livelihood assistance programs

**From DSSD (Social Services):**

- Financial assistance for relocation
- Food packs and relief goods
- Livelihood support
- Social welfare services

---

## 3. Relocation Sites in Bacolod

**Asenso Yuhum Village (Brgy. Vista Alegre):**

- 293 housing units
- For 4Ps beneficiaries and qualified families
- Managed by BHA

**Other Resettlement Sites:**

- Contact BHA for current available sites
- Sites are prepared based on need and availability

---

## 4. Eligibility for Relocation

**Qualified Beneficiaries:**

- Bonafide residents of Bacolod City
- Affected by government projects or living in danger zones
- Indigent families (low-income)
- Willing to relocate to designated sites
- No existing housing unit or lot
- Willing to comply with resettlement rules

**Priority:**

- Families in immediate danger
- Families affected by government infrastructure projects
- 4Ps beneficiaries
- Senior citizens, PWDs, solo parents

---

## 5. How to Apply for Relocation

### Step 1: Verify Eligibility

- Check if your area is affected by demolition or declared danger zone
- Verify through Barangay Hall or BHA

### Step 2: Register with BHA

- Visit **Bacolod Housing Authority**
- Register as affected family
- Submit requirements

### Step 3: Submit Requirements

**Common Requirements:**

- Accomplished application form
- Valid IDs (family members)
- Proof of residency (barangay certificate, utility bills)
- Certificate of Indigency
- Proof of affectation (demolition notice, barangay certification)
- Family composition (birth certificates)

### Step 4: Attend Orientation

- BHA conducts pre-relocation orientation
- Learn about resettlement site rules and regulations
- Sign commitment to relocate

### Step 5: Await Assignment

- BHA evaluates applications
- Housing units are assigned based on priority
- Families are notified of assignment

---

## 6. What to Expect in Relocation Sites

**Basic Amenities:**

- Housing unit (lot and house)
- Access to water and electricity
- Roads and drainage
- Community facilities

**Rules and Responsibilities:**

- Pay monthly amortization (if applicable)
- Maintain cleanliness of unit and surroundings
- Follow community rules
- Participate in community activities
- No selling or renting of units without permission

- **Attend orientations** - required for all beneficiaries
- **Cooperate with authorities** - follow relocation schedule
