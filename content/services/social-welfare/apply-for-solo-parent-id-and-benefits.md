# Solo Parent ID & Benefits — Bacolod City

Solo parents in Bacolod City can avail of benefits, discounts, and assistance programs under **Republic Act 8972 (Solo Parents' Welfare Act)** and local ordinances. The Solo Parent ID provides access to various privileges and support services.

---

## 1) Who is Considered a Solo Parent?

A solo parent is defined as **any individual who falls under the following categories**:

- A woman who gives birth as a result of rape or other crimes against chastity
- Parent left solo due to **death of spouse**
- Parent left solo due to **spouse abandonment** for at least one year
- Parent left solo due to **legal separation or annulment**
- Parent of a child conceived out of wedlock and not living with partner
- Parent left solo due to **imprisonment of spouse** for at least one year
- Parent left solo due to **physical/mental incapacity of spouse** lasting at least one year
- **Unmarried parent** who has preferred to keep and rear the child instead of opting for adoption
- Any other person who **solely provides parental care and support** to a child or children

**Important:** Solo parents must be solely responsible for the care and support of the child/children.

---

## 2) Solo Parent Benefits & Privileges

### Discounts (20%)

Solo parents are entitled to a **20% discount** on:

| Category                | Items/Services                                  | Notes                             |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Medicines**           | Prescription and over-the-counter medicines     | For the solo parent and the child |
| **Medical Services**    | Professional fees, diagnostic tests, lab fees   | In private facilities             |
| **Basic Necessities**   | Rice, bread, corn, milk, vegetables, meat, fish | Up to ₱1,000 per week             |
| **School Tuition Fees** | Private schools from preschool to college       | For the child/children            |

### Work-Related Benefits

- **7 days parental leave** with pay (per year, in addition to regular leave)
- **Flexible work arrangements** (when applicable)
- **Priority in government scholarship programs** for children

### Educational Assistance

- **Scholarship grants** for children of solo parents
- **Tuition fee discounts** at private schools
- **Educational supplies subsidies**

### Housing Benefits

- **Priority in socialized housing programs**
- **Housing loan assistance**

### Livelihood & Skills Training

- **Livelihood programs** and skills training
- **Priority in government employment**
- **Business development assistance**

## 3) How to Apply for Solo Parent ID

### Requirements

1. **Accomplished Solo Parent Application Form** (available at barangay or DSSD)
2. **Proof of Solo Parent Status:**
   - Death Certificate of spouse (if widowed)
   - Court Order (legal separation, annulment, or sole custody)
   - Affidavit of Abandonment (notarized)
   - Affidavit of Sole Responsibility (if unmarried parent)
   - Police Report (if applicable)
   - Birth Certificate of child/children showing solo parent as parent
3. **Valid government-issued ID** (PhilSys, passport, driver's license, etc.)
4. **Birth Certificate of child/children**
5. **1×1 or 2×2 ID photo** (2 copies)
6. **Barangay Clearance** or Certificate of Residency
7. **Certificate of Indigency** (if applicable, for additional assistance)

### Where to Apply

**Step 1: Barangay Level**

1. Go to your **Barangay Hall**
2. Ask for the **Social Services** or **Solo Parent Affairs Desk**
3. Submit requirements
4. Barangay will validate and endorse application to City Social Welfare Office

**Step 2: City Level (DSSD)**

- **Department of Social Services and Development (DSSD)**
- **Email:** dssd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Phone:** (034) 432-1602
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City

### Processing Time

- Barangay endorsement + DSSD validation: **7-14 working days**
- Direct application at DSSD: **10-15 working days**

### Fees

- **FREE** — No application or processing fees

---

## 4) Solo Parent ID Validity & Renewal

- Solo Parent IDs are **valid until the youngest child reaches 18 years old** or graduates from college (whichever comes first)
- Renewal required if solo parent has another child or if ID is lost/damaged
- Must update DSSD if circumstances change (remarriage, reconciliation, etc.)

---

## 5) Additional Assistance Programs for Solo Parents

### Social Pension for Indigent Solo Parents

**Eligibility:**

- Indigent solo parent (no regular income)
- With dependent children

**Benefit:** Cash assistance or subsidies for basic needs

**How to Apply:**

1. Apply at **Barangay Hall** or **DSSD**
2. Submit proof of indigency and Solo Parent ID
3. Wait for assessment and approval

### Livelihood & Skills Training

- DSSD coordinates with TESDA and other agencies for skills training
- Livelihood programs for solo parent entrepreneurs
- Job matching and employment assistance

### Educational Scholarship for Children

- Scholarship programs for children of solo parents
- Priority in government scholarship grants
- Check with DSSD and PESO for available programs

### Legal Assistance

- Free legal aid for solo parents needing legal support
- Assistance with annulment, custody, child support cases
- Referral to Public Attorney's Office (PAO) or city legal office

---

## 6) How to Avail Solo Parent Discounts

### When Making Purchases or Transactions

1. **Present your Solo Parent ID** before payment
2. Inform the cashier/staff that you are availing the solo parent discount
3. Discount will be deducted from your bill
4. Keep receipts for your records

### Tips

- Always carry your Solo Parent ID
- Bring proof of relationship with child (birth certificate) when availing child-related benefits
- Discounts apply to solo parent and dependent children
- If refused, report violations to DSSD or DTI

- **Barangay Officials** — for local mediation
