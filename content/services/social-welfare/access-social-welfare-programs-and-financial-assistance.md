# Social Welfare Programs & Financial Assistance — Bacolod City

The **Department of Social Services and Development (DSSD)** of Bacolod City provides various social welfare programs, financial assistance, and support services for indigent families, vulnerable sectors, and residents in crisis.

---

## 1) Types of Financial Assistance Available

### Medical Assistance

**For:** Indigent patients needing financial help for medical expenses

**Coverage:**

- Hospital bills and confinement expenses
- Laboratory tests and diagnostic procedures
- Medicines and medical supplies
- Dialysis, chemotherapy, radiation therapy
- Surgery and operation costs

**Requirements:**

- Letter of request addressed to the City Mayor or DSSD
- Medical certificate or doctor's prescription
- Hospital bills or cost estimate
- Barangay Certification of Indigency
- Valid IDs (patient and requesting party)
- Social Case Study Report (if required)

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** — Bacolod City Government Center
- **Email:** dssd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Phone:** (034) 432-1602

### Burial Assistance

**For:** Indigent families who need help with funeral and burial expenses

**Coverage:**

- Financial assistance for funeral services
- Casket or burial materials (in some cases)

**Requirements:**

- Death certificate
- Barangay Certification of Indigency
- Funeral service contract or cost estimate
- Valid IDs of claimant
- Proof of relationship to the deceased

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** or **Barangay Hall**

### Emergency Assistance (Calamity, Fire, etc.)

**For:** Families affected by disasters, fires, floods, or other emergencies

**Coverage:**

- Food packs and relief goods
- Temporary shelter assistance
- Cash assistance for rebuilding
- Clothing and basic necessities

**Requirements:**

- Barangay Certification or Fire/Police Report
- Valid IDs
- Proof of residence in affected area

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** or **Barangay Hall**
- **DRRMO (Disaster Risk Reduction Management Office)** for disaster-related assistance

### Transportation Assistance

**For:** Indigent patients needing to travel for medical treatment

**Coverage:**

- Partial or full fare assistance for medical travel
- Ambulance services (coordinate with Bacolod City Health Office)

**Requirements:**

- Medical certificate indicating need for travel
- Barangay Certification of Indigency
- Valid IDs

## 2) Social Welfare Programs

### Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps)

**For:** Extremely poor families with children 0-18 years old or pregnant women

**Benefits:**

- Monthly cash grants for health and education
- Health grants: ₱750/month per family
- Education grants: ₱300-₱500 per child per month (depending on grade level)

**Requirements:**

- Must be listed in the National Household Targeting System for Poverty Reduction (NHTS-PR)
- Children must attend school regularly
- Family must comply with health checkups and vaccinations

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** — Bacolod City Government Center
- Existing 4Ps beneficiaries are automatically enrolled; new applicants must be assessed

### Sustainable Livelihood Program (SLP)

**For:** Unemployed or underemployed individuals seeking livelihood opportunities

**Benefits:**

- Seed capital for micro-enterprises
- Skills training and entrepreneurship training
- Mentorship and business development support

**Requirements:**

- Must be 18-65 years old
- Willing to participate in training and business planning
- Barangay endorsement
- Valid IDs

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** or **PESO (Public Employment Service Office)**
- **Email:** peso@bacolodcity.gov.ph

### Supplementary Feeding Program

**For:** Malnourished children in barangays and daycare centers

**Benefits:**

- Daily nutritious meals for malnourished children
- Nutrition education for parents

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** or **Barangay Nutrition Scholars**

### Community-Based Services for the Elderly

**For:** Senior citizens in need of care and support

**Benefits:**

- Home care services
- Social activities and programs
- Referral to health services

**Where to Apply:**

- **DSSD Office** or **Barangay Senior Citizen Affairs Desk**

## 3) Crisis Intervention Program

**For:** Individuals and families in crisis (abuse, violence, exploitation, emergencies)

**Services:**

- **Crisis counseling** and psychological first aid
- **Temporary shelter** for women and children in distress
- **Referral to legal aid** (domestic violence, abuse cases)
- **Rescue and protective custody** for abused children
- **Livelihood and rehabilitation** support

**Contact:**

- **DSSD Crisis Hotline:** (034) 432-1602
- **24/7 Emergency Hotline:** 911
- **Women and Children Protection Unit (WCPU)** at PNP stations

## 4) How to Apply for Assistance

### General Application Process

1. **Visit your Barangay Hall** for initial endorsement (for indigency certification)
2. **Go to DSSD Office** at Bacolod City Government Center
3. **Submit requirements:**
   - Letter of request (addressed to City Mayor or DSSD Head)
   - Barangay Certification of Indigency
   - Supporting documents (medical certificate, bills, reports, etc.)
   - Valid IDs
4. **Interview and assessment** by DSSD social worker
5. **Wait for approval** (processing time varies by program)
6. **Receive assistance** (cash, goods, or service referral)

### Tips for Faster Processing

- Prepare all documents in advance
- Be honest and complete in your application
- Cooperate with social worker assessment
- Follow up regularly on your application status
