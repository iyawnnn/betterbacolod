# Find Parking Zones, Rules & Apply for Parking Permits — Bacolod City

Bacolod City designates **public parking zones** throughout downtown and commercial areas. Understanding parking rules and securing permits (when needed) helps avoid violations and towing.

---

## 1) Public Parking Zones in Bacolod City

### Downtown & Commercial Areas

**Common parking zones:**

- **Lacson Street** — metered parking zones along commercial strips
- **Araneta Street** — designated parking areas near shops and offices
- **Public Plaza areas** — limited parking available near parks and plazas
- **Shopping malls & commercial centers** — paid parking lots (mall-operated)
- **Government Center** — visitor parking at Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd
- **Market areas** — designated parking zones near public markets

### Parking Types

| Parking Type                | Description                                                | Fees/Requirements                                        |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Metered Street Parking**  | Time-limited parking with payment kiosks or meters         | Hourly rate (varies by zone); typically ₱20-₱40/hour     |
| **Mall/Private Parking**    | Parking lots operated by malls and establishments          | First 3-4 hours free, then ₱20-₱40/hour (varies by mall) |
| **Barangay Parking**        | Community parking areas managed by barangays               | Varies by barangay; some free, some require small fees   |
| **Loading/Unloading Zones** | Designated for short stops only (not for extended parking) | No parking allowed; violators may be ticketed or towed   |

## 2) Parking Rules & Restrictions

### Where You CANNOT Park

❌ **Prohibited Parking Areas:**

- Fire lanes and emergency vehicle zones
- No-parking zones (marked by signs or red curbs)
- Pedestrian crossings and sidewalks
- In front of driveways, gates, or building entrances
- Within 5 meters of fire hydrants
- Loading/unloading zones (unless actively loading/unloading)
- Bus stops and jeepney terminals
- Road obstructions or narrow streets

**Penalty:** Illegal parking can result in fines (₱500 - ₱1,000) and vehicle towing.

### General Parking Rules

✅ **Follow these rules:**

- Always park in designated parking areas only
- Observe parking time limits on metered zones
- Do not block traffic, driveways, or pedestrian paths
- Pay parking fees promptly (for paid zones)
- Display parking tickets or permits visibly on dashboard
- Park within marked lines (do not occupy multiple spaces)

## 3) Parking Permits (For Businesses & Residents)

### Business Parking Permits

If your business needs to reserve parking spaces or operate paid parking:

**Contact:**

- **Bacolod Traffic and Transport Management Division (BTTMD)**
- **Email:** bttmd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City

**Requirements (General):**

- Business registration and Mayor's Permit
- Proof of property ownership or lease agreement
- Site plan showing parking layout
- Barangay Clearance
- Application form and supporting documents

**Process:**

1. Submit application to BTTMD
2. Site inspection and approval
3. Pay permit fees
4. Receive parking permit or authorization

### Residential Parking Permits

Some barangays issue **residential parking permits** for residents in congested areas.

**How to Apply:**

1. Visit your **Barangay Hall**
2. Ask for the Barangay Traffic or Parking desk
3. Submit proof of residence (barangay ID, utility bills, etc.)
4. Pay applicable fees (if any)
5. Receive residential parking sticker or permit

**Note:** Availability varies by barangay. Check with your local barangay office.

## 4) How to Report Illegal Parking

If you see vehicles illegally parked or obstructing traffic:

**Report to:**

- **Bacolod Traffic Authority Office (BTAO)**
- **Email:** btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Provide:** Location, date/time, vehicle plate number, photos (if possible)

**BTAO will:**

- Issue violation tickets
- Tow illegally parked vehicles (if blocking traffic or safety hazards)
- Enforce parking regulations

---

## 5) Vehicle Towing — What to Do

If your vehicle was towed for illegal parking:

### Step 1: Check with BTAO or Towing Office

- **Email:** btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- Ask where your vehicle was towed and impounding location

### Step 2: Settle Fines & Towing Fees

- Go to **City Treasurer's Office** to pay parking violation fine
- Pay towing and impounding fees at the towing office or BTAO

**Contact City Treasurer's Office:**

- **Email:** cto_admin@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Phone:** (034) 435-0785, (034) 434-3983
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center

### Step 3: Claim Your Vehicle

- Bring payment receipts, vehicle registration (OR/CR), valid ID, and driver's license
- Present documents to BTAO or impounding office
- Sign release forms and retrieve your vehicle

**Towing/Impounding Fees:** Varies based on vehicle type and impound duration (check with BTAO)

## 6) Parking Tips for Bacolod Drivers

- **Use mall parking** for longer stays — many offer free parking for first few hours
- **Arrive early** in downtown areas during business hours to find parking
- **Check parking signs** carefully — time limits and restrictions vary by zone
- **Keep small bills/coins** for metered parking payments
- **Avoid parking on narrow streets** or areas with heavy traffic flow
- **Use parking apps** (if available) to find parking spots
- **Respect private parking** — don't park in reserved or private spaces
