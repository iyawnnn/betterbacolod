# Report Traffic Violations, Check Penalties & Settle Traffic Fines — Bacolod City

Bacolod City enforces traffic rules to ensure road safety and smooth traffic flow. Violations are handled by the **Bacolod Traffic Authority Office (BTAO)** and the **Bacolod Traffic and Transport Management Division (BTTMD)** in coordination with the Philippine National Police (PNP).

---

## 1) Common Traffic Violations in Bacolod City

| Violation                            | Description                                                    | Penalty Range                        |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **No Driver's License**              | Driving without a valid license                                | ₱3,000 (first offense)               |
| **Expired Registration**             | Operating a vehicle with expired LTO registration              | ₱10,000 + impoundment                |
| **No Helmet (Motorcycles)**          | Rider or passenger not wearing a helmet                        | ₱1,500 (first offense)               |
| **Illegal Parking**                  | Parking in no-parking zones, fire lanes, pedestrian lanes      | ₱500 - ₱1,000 + towing               |
| **Obstruction**                      | Obstructing traffic flow, loading zones, or public roads       | ₱500 - ₱2,000                        |
| **Reckless Driving**                 | Driving dangerously, speeding, weaving                         | ₱2,000 - ₱5,000 + license suspension |
| **Disregarding Traffic Signs**       | Running red lights, ignoring stop signs, disobeying road signs | ₱1,000 - ₱3,000                      |
| **Drunk Driving**                    | Driving under the influence of alcohol                         | ₱10,000 - ₱20,000 + imprisonment     |
| **Seatbelt Violation**               | Not wearing seatbelt (driver and passengers)                   | ₱1,000                               |
| **Using Mobile Phone While Driving** | Texting or calling without hands-free device                   | ₱1,000 - ₱2,000                      |
| **Smoke Belching**                   | Emitting excessive smoke (violates Clean Air Act)              | ₱2,000 + vehicle impoundment         |
| **Colorum Operations**               | Operating public transport without valid franchise             | ₱200,000 + vehicle impoundment       |

**Note:** Penalties are based on the **Land Transportation and Traffic Code (RA 4136)**, **Anti-Drunk and Drugged Driving Act (RA 10586)**, and local ordinances. Amounts may vary; check with BTAO for current rates.

## 2) How to Report Traffic Violations

### Report to BTAO (Bacolod Traffic Authority Office)

**Email:** btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph
**For:** Traffic violations, illegal parking, obstruction, reckless driving

**How to Report:**

1. **Provide details:**
   - Exact location of violation
   - Date and time
   - Vehicle details (plate number, type, color)
   - Photos or videos (if available)
   - Nature of violation (illegal parking, reckless driving, etc.)

2. **Submit via email** to btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph or visit the office in person

3. **Follow up** — BTAO will investigate and take enforcement action

### Report to BTTMD (Traffic Management Division)

**Email:** bttmd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
**Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City

**For:** Traffic management concerns, route violations, PUV issues, road hazards

### Report to PNP (Philippine National Police)

**For:** Serious violations (drunk driving, reckless driving causing accidents, hit-and-run)
**Hotline:** 911 or local PNP station

## 3) How to Check Traffic Violations & Pay Fines

### Step 1: Check if You Have Pending Violations

**Options:**

- **LTO Portal:** Visit [lto.gov.ph](https://lto.gov.ph) and use the "Check Violations" service (if available online)
- **Visit LTO Bacolod Office:** Bring your OR/CR and driver's license to check for pending violations
- **Contact BTAO:** Email btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph with your plate number and ask if there are pending city violations

### Step 2: Settle Traffic Fines

**Where to Pay:**

1. **Bacolod City Treasurer's Office** (for city-issued violations)
   - **Email:** cto_admin@bacolodcity.gov.ph
   - **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte
   - Bring your Temporary Operator's Permit (TOP) or violation ticket

2. **LTO Bacolod** (for LTO-issued violations)
   - Pay at LTO cashier
   - Bring violation ticket and valid ID

3. **Court (for serious violations)**
   - Some violations require court appearance
   - Check your violation ticket for instructions

### Step 3: Retrieve Your License or Documents

- After paying the fine, retrieve your confiscated license from the issuing office
- Keep your Official Receipt (OR) as proof of payment

## 4) Impounded Vehicles — How to Claim

If your vehicle was impounded due to a traffic violation:

**Process:**

1. **Go to BTAO or the impounding office**
   - Email: btao@bacolodcity.gov.ph
   - Bring your vehicle registration (OR/CR), driver's license, and ID

2. **Settle all fines and penalties**
   - Pay violation fines at the City Treasurer's Office
   - Pay impounding/towing fees at the impounding office

3. **Present payment receipts** and claim your vehicle

**Impounding Fees:**

- Varies based on vehicle type and number of days impounded
- Check with BTAO for current rates

## 5) Traffic Safety & Compliance Tips

- **Always carry** your driver's license, OR, CR, and insurance when driving
- **Follow traffic rules** — road signs, speed limits, traffic lights
- **Wear helmets** (motorcycles) and seatbelts (cars) at all times
- **Avoid drunk driving** — use public transport or designated drivers
- **Keep vehicles roadworthy** — valid registration, no smoke belching, functional lights
- **Don't use mobile phones** while driving unless hands-free
- **Obey parking rules** — park only in designated areas
