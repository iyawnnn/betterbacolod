# Apply for Transport Permits, Franchise Registration & Vehicle Clearances — Bacolod City

Operating public utility vehicles (PUVs) such as jeepneys, tricycles, taxis, or buses in Bacolod City requires **transport franchises** from national agencies and **local clearances** from the city government.

---

## 1) Types of Transport Permits & Franchises

| Type                             | Issued By                                                | Purpose                                                             |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Franchise for Public Utility** | LTFRB (Land Transportation Franchising Regulatory Board) | Required for jeepneys, buses, taxis, UVs to operate commercially    |
| **Mayor's Permit for PUV**       | Bacolod City BPLO (Business Permits & Licensing Office)  | Annual city clearance for PUV operators                             |
| **Tricycle Franchise**           | Barangay + City Transport Office                         | Franchise to operate tricycles within specific barangay zones       |
| **Traffic/Transport Clearance**  | BTTMD (Bacolod Traffic & Transport Management Division)  | Clearance for vehicle operations, route compliance                  |
| **LTO Registration & OR/CR**     | LTO (Land Transportation Office)                         | Vehicle registration, official receipt, certificate of registration |

---

## 2) How to Apply for Public Utility Vehicle (PUV) Franchise

### Step 1: Apply for LTFRB Franchise (National Level)

**LTFRB (Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board)** is the national agency that grants franchises for jeepneys, buses, taxis, and other PUVs.

**Requirements (General):**

- Accomplished application form
- Proof of vehicle ownership (OR/CR from LTO)
- Certificate of Public Convenience (CPC) or transfer of rights
- Mayor's Permit or Business Permit
- Barangay Clearance
- Route study or service plan (if new route)
- Other documents as required by LTFRB

**Where to Apply:**

- **LTFRB Region 6 Office** — Iloilo City (serving Western Visayas including Bacolod)
- **Website:** [ltfrb.gov.ph](https://ltfrb.gov.ph)

**Timeline:** Processing time varies; check with LTFRB for current timelines

---

### Step 2: Secure Mayor's Permit for PUV (Bacolod City)

After obtaining your LTFRB franchise, you must secure a **Mayor's Permit** from the Bacolod City government.

**Where to Apply:**

- **Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO)**
- **Email:** bplo@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City
- **Website:** [bacolodcity.gov.ph](https://bacolodcity.gov.ph/business-permits-and-licensing-division/)

**Requirements:**

- Valid LTFRB franchise certificate
- LTO Certificate of Registration (CR)
- Barangay Clearance
- Cedula (Community Tax Certificate)
- Business registration documents (if operating as a business entity)
- Other city requirements (fire safety clearance, sanitary permit if applicable)

**Process:**

1. Submit requirements to BPLO
2. Pay applicable fees and local taxes
3. Receive Mayor's Permit for the year
4. Renew annually

## 3) Tricycle Franchise Application

Tricycle operations are regulated at the **barangay and city levels**.

### How to Apply

1. **Go to your Barangay Hall**
   - Ask for the Barangay Transport or Tricycle Association desk
   - Each barangay may have specific requirements and franchise zones

2. **Prepare Requirements:**
   - LTO registration of tricycle (OR/CR)
   - Barangay Clearance
   - Cedula
   - Proof of residence in the barangay (if required)
   - Membership in Barangay Tricycle Operators and Drivers Association (TODA), if applicable

3. **Secure City-Level Clearance (if required)**
   - Contact **BTTMD (Bacolod Traffic & Transport Management Division)**
   - Email: bttmd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
   - May require route compliance or zone clearance

4. **Pay franchise fees** and receive franchise certificate/sticker

### Important Notes

- Tricycle franchises are **zone-specific** — you can only operate within designated barangay zones
- Franchise fees vary by barangay
- Renewal is typically annual

## 4) Vehicle Registration & Clearances (LTO)

### Land Transportation Office (LTO) — Bacolod

**Services:**

- Vehicle registration (new and renewal)
- Driver's license application and renewal
- Plate issuance
- Official Receipt (OR) and Certificate of Registration (CR)

**LTO Bacolod Office:**

- **Address:** Check [lto.gov.ph](https://lto.gov.ph) for exact location and contact details
- **Website:** [lto.gov.ph](https://lto.gov.ph)

**Requirements for Vehicle Registration:**

- Certificate of Registration (CR) or Sales Invoice (for new vehicles)
- Official Receipt (OR)
- Insurance Certificate (Compulsory Third Party Liability - CTPL)
- Emission Test Certificate
- Other documents as required

**Timeline:** Same-day processing for renewals (if no pending issues)

---

## 5) Traffic & Transport Clearance (BTTMD)

For route compliance, transport operations, and traffic clearances:

**Bacolod Traffic and Transport Management Division (BTTMD)**

- **Email:** bttmd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City
- **For:** Route clearances, traffic management, PUV operations coordination

---

## 6) Annual Renewal Requirements

**All PUV operators must renew annually:**

- **LTFRB Franchise renewal** — at LTFRB office
- **Mayor's Permit renewal** — at BPLO Bacolod
- **LTO vehicle registration** — at LTO Bacolod
- **Barangay Clearance** (if applicable)

**Important:** Operating without valid permits and franchises can result in fines, penalties, and impoundment of vehicles.

---

## 7) Tips for PUV Operators

- Start renewal process **1-2 months before expiry** to avoid lapses
- Keep all documents (OR, CR, franchise certificates) in the vehicle at all times
- Comply with **fare rates** approved by LTFRB
- Follow **route assignments** strictly — deviation can result in penalties
- Maintain vehicle roadworthiness and cleanliness
- Join your local transport organization for updates and support
