# Disaster Preparedness & Emergency Planning — Bacolod City

Bacolod City faces natural disasters including typhoons, floods, earthquakes, and fires. The **Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (DRRMO)** coordinates disaster preparedness, response, and recovery efforts to keep residents safe.

---

## 1) Common Disasters in Bacolod City

### Typhoons & Floods

- **Season:** June to December (rainy season, peak August-October)
- **Risk:** Strong winds, heavy rains, flooding in low-lying areas
- **Preparation:** Stock food, water, secure homes, know evacuation routes

### Earthquakes

- **Risk:** Negros Island is in a seismically active zone
- **Preparation:** Drop-Cover-Hold drills, secure heavy furniture, emergency kit

### Fires

- **Risk:** Urban fires in residential and commercial areas
- **Preparation:** Fire extinguishers, clear fire exits, smoke detectors

### Health Emergencies & Pandemics

- **Risk:** Dengue, COVID-19, other infectious diseases
- **Preparation:** Hygiene, vaccinations, health supplies

---

## 2) How to Prepare for Disasters

### Build an Emergency Kit

Every household should have a **72-hour emergency kit** (3 days of supplies):

**Food & Water:**

- Drinking water (1 gallon per person per day)
- Non-perishable food (canned goods, instant noodles, biscuits)
- Manual can opener

**Medical Supplies:**

- First aid kit
- Prescription medicines (at least 1 week supply)
- Over-the-counter medicines (pain relievers, anti-diarrhea)
- Vitamins and supplements

**Documents & Money:**

- Photocopies of IDs (PhilSys, birth certificates, passports)
- Land titles, insurance papers
- Cash (ATMs may not work during disasters)
- Store in waterproof container or ziplock bags

**Tools & Supplies:**

- Flashlight and extra batteries
- Battery-powered radio or AM/FM radio
- Mobile phone charger (powerbank fully charged)
- Matches or lighter in waterproof container
- Whistle (to signal for help)
- Duct tape and plastic sheeting

**Clothing & Hygiene:**

- Change of clothes per family member
- Blankets or sleeping bags
- Soap, toothbrush, toothpaste
- Feminine hygiene products
- Garbage bags

**Special Needs:**

- Baby formula, diapers (if you have infants)
- Pet food and supplies
- Eyeglasses, hearing aids
- Mobility aids (canes, wheelchairs)

## 3) Know Your Evacuation Plan

### Find Your Nearest Evacuation Center

Every barangay in Bacolod City has designated **evacuation centers**:

- Schools (gymnasiums, classrooms)
- Barangay halls
- Covered courts
- Community centers

**Check with your Barangay Hall** to confirm your assigned evacuation center.

### Plan Your Evacuation Route

1. Know **at least 2 routes** to your evacuation center (in case one is blocked)
2. Practice the route with your family
3. Identify safe meeting points if family members are separated

### What to Bring to Evacuation Centers

- Emergency kit (food, water, medicines)
- Important documents
- Change of clothes and blankets
- Personal hygiene items
- Mobile phone and charger

## 4) During a Disaster

### Typhoon & Flood

**Before:**

- Monitor weather updates from PAGASA and DRRMO
- Secure outdoor items (furniture, plants)
- Stock up on food, water, and supplies
- Charge phones and devices
- Fill bathtubs and containers with water (in case water supply is cut)

**During:**

- Stay indoors, away from windows
- Do not wade through floodwaters (risk of electrocution, disease)
- Follow evacuation orders from authorities
- Stay tuned to updates via radio or official social media

**After:**

- Wait for authorities to declare "all clear" before returning home
- Inspect home for damage (structural, electrical, gas leaks)
- Avoid downed power lines
- Report damage to barangay or DRRMO

### Earthquake

**During:**

- **Drop, Cover, Hold** — get under a sturdy table or desk
- Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy objects
- If outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, and power lines
- If in a vehicle, stop safely and stay inside

**After:**

- Expect aftershocks — be ready to drop, cover, hold again
- Check for injuries and provide first aid
- Evacuate if building is damaged
- Do not use elevators
- Report to evacuation centers if home is unsafe

### Fire

**Before:**

- Install smoke detectors
- Keep fire extinguishers accessible
- Plan fire escape routes with family
- Practice fire drills

**During:**

- Call 911 immediately
- Evacuate using fire exits (do not use elevators)
- Crawl low under smoke
- Once out, do not go back inside

## 6) Early Warning Systems

### Storm Signals (PAGASA)

- **Signal No. 1:** Winds 30-60 km/h expected within 36 hours
- **Signal No. 2:** Winds 61-120 km/h expected within 24 hours
- **Signal No. 3:** Winds 121-170 km/h expected within 18 hours
- **Signal No. 4:** Winds 171-220 km/h expected within 12 hours
- **Signal No. 5:** Winds exceeding 220 km/h expected within 12 hours

**Automatic Suspension of Classes & Work:**

- Signal No. 2 and above: Automatic suspension (refer to DRRMO/local government announcements)

### Flood Warnings

- DRRMO issues flood warnings for low-lying areas
- Evacuate immediately when advised
