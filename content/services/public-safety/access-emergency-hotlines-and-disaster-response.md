## 2) What to Do in an Emergency

### Fire Emergency

1. **Call 911 or (034) 432-3871** immediately
2. Evacuate the building — use fire exits, do not use elevators
3. Alert others — shout "Fire!" to warn occupants
4. If possible, use fire extinguisher (only if fire is small and you are trained)
5. Do not re-enter the building until authorities declare it safe

### Medical Emergency

1. **Call 911** for ambulance
2. Provide exact location and nature of emergency
3. Stay with the patient and provide first aid if trained
4. Keep patient calm and comfortable
5. Follow dispatcher's instructions

### Crime or Police Emergency

1. **Call 911** immediately
2. Provide location, description of suspect/incident
3. Stay safe — do not confront criminals
4. Preserve evidence (if safe to do so)
5. Cooperate with responding officers

### Natural Disaster (Typhoon, Flood, Earthquake)

1. **Call DRRMO hotlines:** (034) 432-3871 to 73 or +63 930 243 4706
2. Follow evacuation orders from authorities
3. Move to designated evacuation centers
4. Bring emergency supplies (food, water, medicines, documents)
5. Stay tuned to official announcements (radio, TV, social media)

## 3) Disaster Preparedness & Evacuation Centers

### Evacuation Centers

Bacolod City has designated **evacuation centers** in every barangay for typhoons, floods, and other disasters:

- Schools (gymnasiums, classrooms)
- Barangay halls and covered courts
- Community centers

**Check with your barangay hall** for the nearest evacuation center in your area.

### Disaster Preparedness Tips

**Before a Disaster:**

- Prepare an **emergency kit** (food, water, flashlight, batteries, first aid, medicines)
- Keep **important documents** in waterproof containers (IDs, birth certificates, land titles)
- Know your **evacuation routes** and nearest evacuation center
- Charge phones and powerbanks
- Store drinking water and non-perishable food

**During a Disaster:**

- Follow official evacuation orders
- Stay indoors during typhoons and floods
- Avoid flooded areas and downed power lines
- Tune in to official updates (DRRMO, local government)

**After a Disaster:**

- Wait for authorities to declare areas safe
- Report injuries, damages, or trapped persons to DRRMO
- Avail of relief goods and assistance from barangay or DSSD
