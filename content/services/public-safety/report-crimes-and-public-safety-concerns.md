# Report Crimes & Public Safety Concerns — Bacolod City

Residents of Bacolod City can report crimes, suspicious activities, and public safety concerns to the **Philippine National Police (PNP)**, **Barangay Officials**, and other authorities to maintain peace and order in the community.

---

## 1) Types of Crimes & Concerns to Report

### Crimes Requiring Immediate Police Response (Call 911)

- **Robbery, theft, hold-up** (in progress or just occurred)
- **Assault, physical violence**
- **Murder, homicide**
- **Kidnapping, abduction**
- **Rape, sexual assault**
- **Domestic violence** (physical abuse, threats)
- **Drug-related activities** (drug dealing, drug use)
- **Illegal firearms possession**

### Non-Emergency Crimes & Concerns

- **Property disputes** (boundary issues, trespassing)
- **Threats and harassment** (verbal threats, cyberbullying)
- **Lost or stolen property** (after the fact)
- **Noise complaints** (loud parties, karaoke past curfew)
- **Stray animals** (dangerous dogs, animal cruelty)
- **Illegal dumping** (trash in public spaces)
- **Suspicious activities** (loitering, unfamiliar persons)

---

## 2) How to Report Crimes

### Emergency: Call 911 Immediately

**For crimes in progress or life-threatening situations:**

1. **Dial 911** from any phone (mobile or landline)
2. Provide:
   - Your exact location (address, landmarks)
   - Nature of the emergency (robbery, assault, etc.)
   - Description of suspect (height, clothing, vehicle)
   - Number of suspects or injured persons
3. Stay on the line and follow dispatcher's instructions
4. **Stay safe** — do not confront criminals

**911 is available 24/7 nationwide**

---

### Non-Emergency: Report to Barangay or Police Station

**For non-urgent concerns:**

**Step 1: Go to your Barangay Hall**

- Report the incident to **Barangay Officials** or **Barangay Tanod**
- Barangay can mediate minor disputes (property, noise, neighborhood issues)
- For crimes, barangay will assist in filing a report with police

**Step 2: File a Police Report**

- Visit the nearest **PNP Police Station**
- Bring valid IDs and any evidence (photos, videos, witnesses)
- File a **Blotter Report** or formal complaint
- Get a copy of the police report for your records

---

## 3) Reporting Domestic Violence & Abuse

### Women and Children Protection Unit (WCPU)

**For cases involving:**

- Domestic violence (physical, emotional, economic abuse)
- Violence against women (RA 9262 - VAWC)
- Child abuse and neglect
- Trafficking of women and children

**How to Report:**

1. **Call 911** for immediate assistance
2. Visit **WCPU at PNP Police Stations**
3. Contact **DSSD (Department of Social Services and Development)**
   - Phone: (034) 432-1602
   - Email: dssd@bacolodcity.gov.ph
4. Seek help from **barangay Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) desk**

**Services Available:**

- Protective custody for victims
- Referral to temporary shelters
- Legal assistance and counseling
- Barangay or court protection orders

**Free Legal Aid:**

- **Public Attorney's Office (PAO)** — free legal services for indigent victims
- **Integrated Bar of the Philippines (IBP)** — pro bono legal aid

---

## 4) Reporting Drug-Related Activities

### How to Report

**For drug dealing, drug dens, or drug use:**

1. **Call 911** or report to **PNP Anti-Drug Unit**
2. Visit the nearest police station and file a report
3. **Anonymous reporting:** Call PNP hotlines or use barangay reporting systems
4. Provide as much detail as possible (location, time, suspects)

**Safety Tips:**

- Do not confront suspected drug dealers
- Protect your identity — request anonymity if concerned about safety
- Let authorities handle the investigation

---

## 5) Reporting Suspicious Activities

### What to Report

- Unfamiliar persons loitering in your area
- Vehicles with no clear purpose parked for extended periods
- Unusual activities at odd hours
- Persons asking suspicious questions about homes or businesses

### How to Report

1. **Inform your Barangay Tanod** or Barangay Officials
2. Call the **PNP hotline** or visit the police station
3. Provide descriptions (person, vehicle, location, time)

**Community Safety:**

- Join **Barangay Watch Groups** or **Ronda** (neighborhood patrols)
- Coordinate with neighbors for mutual vigilance

---

## 6) Reporting Public Safety Hazards

### What to Report

- **Road hazards:** Potholes, damaged streetlights, blocked roads
- **Fire hazards:** Illegal electrical connections, combustible materials
- **Structural hazards:** Damaged buildings, unstable structures
- **Environmental hazards:** Chemical spills, hazardous waste

## 7) What Happens After Reporting

### Police Response

- **Emergency (911):** Police patrol will be dispatched immediately
- **Non-emergency:** Police will investigate and follow up
- **You may be asked** to provide a formal statement or affidavit

### Investigation & Follow-Up

- Police will investigate the crime or concern
- You may be contacted for additional information
- Keep your police report number for reference

### Legal Action

- For crimes, the case may proceed to **Prosecutor's Office** for filing of charges
- Victims may need to testify in court
- Seek assistance from PAO for free legal representation
