# Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (FSIC) — Bacolod City

Businesses, establishments, and structures in Bacolod City must secure a **Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (FSIC)** from the **Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP)** as required by **Republic Act 9514 (Fire Code of the Philippines)** and for business permit renewal.

---

## 1) What is a Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (FSIC)?

A **Fire Safety Inspection Certificate** is a document issued by the BFP certifying that a building, structure, or establishment complies with **fire safety standards** and regulations under the Fire Code.

**Required for:**

- Business permit applications and renewals
- New building occupancy permits
- Major renovations or changes in building use
- Schools, hospitals, malls, hotels, and other public establishments

---

## 2) Who Needs a Fire Safety Inspection Certificate?

### Mandatory for All:

- **Commercial establishments** (stores, restaurants, offices)
- **Industrial facilities** (factories, warehouses)
- **Institutional buildings** (schools, hospitals, churches)
- **High-rise buildings** (residential condominiums, commercial towers)
- **Entertainment venues** (cinemas, bars, KTVs)
- **Hotels and lodging houses**
- **Public assembly areas** (convention centers, gyms, markets)

### Exemptions:

- Single-family residential houses (unless operating a business)
- Small home-based businesses (check with BFP for threshold)

---

## 3) Fire Safety Requirements

### General Fire Safety Compliance

To pass BFP inspection, establishments must have:

- **Fire exits** — clearly marked, unobstructed, and accessible
- **Fire extinguishers** — properly placed, maintained, and inspected
- **Emergency lights** — functional and strategically located
- **Fire alarms and smoke detectors** (for larger establishments)
- **Exit signs** — illuminated and visible
- **Fire hose cabinets** (for multi-story buildings)
- **Sprinkler systems** (for high-rise buildings and large establishments)
- **Electrical safety** — proper wiring, no overloading, grounded outlets
- **Flammable materials storage** — proper containment and signage

### For Specific Types of Buildings:

- **Schools & Hospitals:** Fire drills, evacuation plans, dedicated fire safety officer
- **Hotels & High-Rise Buildings:** Standpipe systems, fire pumps, pressurized fire escape stairs
- **Factories & Warehouses:** Hazardous materials permits, industrial fire suppression systems

---

## 4) How to Apply for FSIC

### Step 1: Prepare Your Establishment

1. Ensure all fire safety equipment is in place and functional
2. Clear all fire exits and emergency routes
3. Install required fire extinguishers (check BFP guidelines for quantity and type)
4. Post emergency evacuation maps and contact numbers
5. Conduct internal fire safety check

### Step 2: Secure Requirements

**Documents Needed:**

- **Accomplished FSIC Application Form** (available at BFP Bacolod or online)
- **Building Permit** or **Occupancy Permit** (from Office of Building Officials)
- **Floor Plan or Layout** — showing fire exits, extinguisher locations, emergency routes
- **Business Permit** (if renewing) or business registration documents
- **Barangay Clearance**
- **Proof of Occupancy** (lease contract, deed of sale, or tax declaration)
- **Previous FSIC** (if renewal)

### Step 3: Visit BFP Bacolod

- **Bureau of Fire Protection - Bacolod City**
- **Phone:** (034) 434-9122, (034) 432-7196
- **Location:** Bacolod City Government Center, Circumferential Rd, Brgy. Villamonte, Bacolod City, 6100 Negros Occidental
- **Website:** [bacolodcity.gov.ph/bureau-of-fire-protection](https://bacolodcity.gov.ph/bureau-of-fire-protection/)

**Submit requirements and schedule fire safety inspection**

### Step 4: BFP Inspection

- BFP fire marshal will visit your establishment
- Inspects fire safety equipment, exits, electrical systems, and compliance
- May issue **Notice of Violation** if deficiencies are found
- You must correct violations before FSIC is issued

### Step 5: Pay Fees & Receive FSIC

- Pay FSIC fees at BFP or City Treasurer's Office
- Fees vary based on building type, size, and classification
- Receive Fire Safety Inspection Certificate

**Processing Time:** 3-7 working days (if no violations)

---

## 5) FSIC Validity & Renewal

- **Validity:** 1 year (must be renewed annually)
- **Renewal:** Submit renewal application before expiration
- **Requirements:** Same as initial application + previous FSIC
- **Inspection:** BFP may conduct re-inspection for renewals

**Tip:** Start renewal process 1-2 months before expiration to avoid business permit delays.

---

## 6) Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Operating Without FSIC

- Business permit will **not be issued or renewed**
- **Fines and penalties** under RA 9514 (Fire Code)
- Possible **closure order** for serious fire hazards
- Criminal liability for owners/managers if fire occurs due to negligence

### Fire Safety Violations

- **Notice of Violation** — must be corrected within specified period
- **Fines** — for each violation (amounts vary)
- **Closure** — for establishments with serious fire hazards

---

## 7) Fire Safety Tips for Establishments

### Maintenance

- **Inspect fire extinguishers monthly** — check pressure gauge, accessibility
- **Test emergency lights** — ensure batteries are functional
- **Conduct fire drills** — at least twice a year
- **Keep fire exits clear** — no storage or obstructions
- **Check electrical wiring** — repair frayed wires, avoid overloading

### Training

- Designate a **fire safety officer** or committee
- Train employees on fire extinguisher use
- Post emergency contact numbers prominently
- Maintain evacuation plans and update regularly

### Reporting Fire Hazards

- Report fire hazards to **BFP Bacolod** or **DRRMO**
- Call **911** or fire emergency hotline for active fires
